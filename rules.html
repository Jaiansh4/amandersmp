Discord Rules

These rules keep the community safe and welcoming. Staff decisions are final; appeals can be made through a support ticket.

  1. Be Respectful — Treat everyone with respect. No insults, bullying, or harassment. Repeated violations may lead to mute or ban.
  2. No Spamming — Avoid repeated messages, mentions, or unnecessary pings. Use threads and channels appropriately.
  3. No NSFW or Offensive Content — Keep the community appropriate for all ages. Sharing explicit or hateful content is prohibited.
  4. No Self-Promotion — Do not promote other servers, links, or YouTube channels without permission from staff.
  5. Use Channels Properly — Keep discussions relevant to each channel’s topic. Off-topic posts may be moved or removed.
  6. Respect Staff Decisions — Moderators and administrators enforce rules. If you disagree, open a ticket for review.

Minecraft Rules

Follow these rules in-game. Breaking them can result in warnings, temporary bans, or permanent bans depending on severity.

  1. No Griefing or Stealing — Do not destroy, loot, or interfere with other players’ builds or items. Respect claims and trusted areas.
  2. No Hacking or Exploiting — Any cheating (X-ray, duping, auto-clickers, macros) is strictly prohibited. Evidence-based bans will be enforced.
  3. No PvP Without Consent — Do not attack players unless they agree or during designated PvP events.
  4. Protect Private Areas — Use claims or trust systems to protect your base. Respect other players’ protected land.
  5. No Lag-Causing Builds or Farms — Avoid redstone contraptions or farms that cause server lag. Staff may request changes to reduce impact.
  6. Respect the Community — Be cooperative and helpful. Harassment, doxxing, or targeted abuse will be punished.

Reporting and Appeals

Report rule violations by opening a ticket in Discord with evidence (screenshots, timestamps, player names). Appeals for punishments should be submitted through the support ticket system and will be reviewed by staff.
